About

About Me:

I'm a 3rd year student at the Rochester Institute of Technology. I'm studying software engineering with a minor in computer science and communications. I have passion for self improvement and I believe that this shows in my work. I've taught myself the basics of a web framework (literally) overnight and have spent most of my time learning how to learn quicker, faster, and more effectively. I have two years experience building web applications for companies in the health domain.

Methodology

Agile:

I subscribe to the philosophy that it's impossible to know all of the ins and outs of a finished project before building it. Because of this I follow an agile methodology allowing changes in the design to be easily made. In addition, I use tools to ensure that time can be spent developing and fixing issues without worrying about one small change breaking and entire ssytem.

Pomodoros is a form of time management to easily measure out work that can be done by looking at previous work. It is a form of time-boxing to see how time can be used most efficiently. Because I use this technique, it's very easy to guage how long tasks should take along with being able to analyze weak points in my process and make improvements in the future.

Refactoring with Rubocop

Refactoring with Rubocop is ruby challenge intended to help teach the concepts of metaprogramming and refactoring to newer ruby users. The challenge brings you along a path of refactoring a project and refactoring bad code. In addition, there are elements of TDD available for newer users to familiarize themselves with the value of TDD.
